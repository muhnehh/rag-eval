# Working Capital Finance Notes

## Accounts Receivable vs Accounts Payable
Accounts receivable is money owed to the company by customers after a sale on credit. Accounts payable is money the company owes to suppliers for goods or services already received.

## Credit Limits
A credit limit is the maximum balance a company is willing to let a customer owe at one time. Credit limits are set using payment history, financial strength, and risk assessment. If a customer exceeds the limit, new orders may be blocked until payment is received.

## Invoice Discounting
Invoice discounting is a financing method where a business borrows against unpaid customer invoices. It improves short-term liquidity by turning receivables into cash before the customer actually pays.

## Remittance Advice
Remittance advice is a message or document from the payer that explains which invoices are included in a payment and whether any short-payments or deductions were taken. It helps the seller apply cash correctly.

## Collection Performance
High DSO is usually caused by slow-paying customers, invoice disputes, weak follow-up, billing errors, or loose credit policies. AR teams monitor these patterns to reduce overdue balances and improve cash conversion.
