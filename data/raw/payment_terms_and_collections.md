# Payment Terms and Collections

## Net Terms
Net 30 means the full invoice amount is due within 30 days of the invoice date. Net 60 and Net 90 extend the due date further and increase the buyer's time to pay.

## Early Payment Discounts
The term 2/10 net 30 means the buyer may deduct 2% if payment is made within 10 days; otherwise the full amount is due in 30 days. Early payment discounts encourage faster payment and can improve supplier cash flow.

## Aging Reports
An accounts receivable aging report groups open invoices by how long they have been unpaid. Common buckets are:
- 0-30 days
- 31-60 days
- 61-90 days
- Over 90 days

Aging reports help teams focus collection efforts and estimate bad debt risk.

## Dunning Workflow
A dunning workflow is a sequence of collection reminders for overdue invoices. It often starts with a friendly reminder, then escalates to stronger notices and eventually a formal demand or external collections if the account remains unpaid.
