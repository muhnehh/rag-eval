# Accounts Receivable Guide

## What is Accounts Receivable?
Accounts receivable (AR) represents the balance of money due to a firm from its customers for goods or services delivered but not yet paid for. It is recorded as a current asset on the balance sheet.

## Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
DSO measures the average number of days it takes a company to collect payment after a sale. The formula is:
- **DSO = (Accounts Receivable / Total Credit Sales) × Number of Days**

A DSO under 45 days is generally considered healthy. A high DSO indicates slow collections and cash flow problems.

## Invoice Aging
Invoice aging categorizes unpaid invoices by how overdue they are:
- Current: 0-30 days past due
- 1-30 days past due
- 31-60 days past due  
- 61-90 days past due
- Over 90 days past due

## Dunning Workflow
A dunning workflow is a systematic process of sending payment reminders:
1. Day 1-7: Friendly reminder email
2. Day 15: Second notice with late fee warning
3. Day 30: Formal demand letter
4. Day 60+: Escalation to collections or legal action

## Three-Way Matching
Three-way matching in accounts payable compares:
- Purchase Order (PO)
- Goods Receipt Note (GRN)
- Vendor Invoice

All three must agree on quantities, prices, and terms before payment is released.

## Payment Terms
Common payment terms include:
- Net 30: Payment due within 30 days
- Net 60: Payment due within 60 days
- 2/10 Net 30: 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise full amount due in 30 days

## GRNI (Goods Received Not Invoiced)
GRNI represents inventory received but not yet invoiced. It creates a liability on the balance sheet. The accounting entry debits inventory and credits GRNI accrual.
