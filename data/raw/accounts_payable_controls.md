# Accounts Payable Controls

## Purchase Orders and Procure-to-Pay
A purchase order (PO) is a formal document sent by a buyer to a vendor that specifies goods or services, quantities, prices, and agreed terms. It creates an authorized commitment to buy.

The procure-to-pay (P2P) process covers the full cycle:
- Identify a purchase need
- Create and approve a purchase requisition
- Issue a purchase order
- Receive goods or confirm services
- Match the invoice to supporting documents
- Pay the vendor

## Three-Way Matching
Three-way matching verifies that three records agree before payment:
- Purchase order
- Goods receipt note or receiving record
- Vendor invoice

This control helps prevent duplicate payments, fraud, and payment for items not ordered or not received.

## GRNI
GRNI stands for Goods Received Not Invoiced. It is an accrual used when goods or services have been received but the supplier invoice has not arrived yet. The balance should be reviewed at period end so liabilities are not understated.

## Vendor Reconciliation
Vendor reconciliation compares the company's AP ledger with the vendor statement. The goal is to identify missing invoices, disputed items, timing differences, and duplicate payments before close or payment runs.
